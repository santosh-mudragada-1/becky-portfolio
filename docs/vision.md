
# Vision

## Project Name

Interactive Product Manager & Marketing Portfolio

---

# Purpose

This is not a traditional portfolio website.

It is an interactive product experience designed to showcase product thinking, marketing strategy, creativity, and personality. Every interaction should demonstrate how I approach problems, communicate ideas, and build products—not just display my work.

Visitors should feel like they are exploring a product rather than reading a portfolio.

---

# Goal

The portfolio should make recruiters remember me.

Instead of simply telling people that I'm creative, strategic, and user-focused, the website should let them experience those qualities through storytelling, interactions, and playful moments.

The goal is to create a portfolio that people enjoy exploring and want to share with others.

---

# Target Audience

Primary Audience

* Startup founders
* Product leaders
* Hiring managers
* Venture-backed companies
* Product-first organizations

Dream Companies

* Social Capital
* Offbeat
* M.A.N.G.O.

---

# Brand Personality

The personality should feel like:

* Playful
* Clever
* Witty
* Curious
* Slightly chaotic
* Self-aware
* Friendly
* Confident
* Creative
* Approachable

The humor should come from real product management experiences instead of random internet memes.

---

# Emotional Journey

The visitor should experience the following emotions while exploring the website:

1. Curiosity
2. Surprise
3. Laughter
4. Interest
5. Trust
6. Admiration
7. "I want to interview this person."

Every section should increase curiosity rather than simply present information.

---

# Core Principles

## Show, Don't Tell

Avoid writing long paragraphs explaining skills.

Instead, demonstrate product thinking through interactions, decisions, dashboards, experiments, and storytelling.

---

## Every Section Should Feel Alive

Nothing should feel static.

Buttons should respond.

Cards should animate.

Illustrations should react.

Microinteractions should reward curiosity.

---

## Build a Product, Not a Portfolio

Visitors shouldn't feel like they're scrolling through another resume.

The experience should resemble a polished digital product.

---

## Story Before Screenshots

Projects should tell a complete story:

Problem

↓

Research

↓

Insights

↓

Decision Making

↓

Execution

↓

Impact

↓

Reflection

The focus should always be on thinking, not just visuals.

---

## Personality Matters

Professional doesn't have to mean boring.

The website should comfortably include:

* Funny observations
* Product jokes
* Self-aware humor
* Playful copy
* Creative illustrations
* Easter eggs

Humor should enhance the experience, never distract from it.

---

# Visual Direction

The visual style should feel like a creative product team's workspace.

Inspiration:

* Sticky notes
* Whiteboards
* Coffee stains
* Sketches
* Doodles
* Product roadmaps
* Kanban boards
* User journey maps
* Product dashboards

The experience should feel handcrafted rather than corporate.

Avoid a minimal Apple-inspired aesthetic.

---

# Interaction Philosophy

Interactions should feel rewarding.

Visitors should discover things naturally.

Examples include:

* Hover animations
* Hidden jokes
* Interactive product decisions
* Dashboard widgets
* Unlockable achievements
* Keyboard shortcuts
* Theme switching
* Small surprises throughout the experience

The website should encourage exploration.

---

# Tone of Voice

Writing should be:

* Conversational
* Confident
* Clear
* Short
* Friendly
* Smart
* Occasionally sarcastic

Avoid:

* Corporate buzzwords
* Long paragraphs
* Generic motivational quotes
* Overused startup clichés

---

# Success Criteria

The portfolio is successful if visitors:

* Spend several minutes exploring the site.
* Interact with multiple sections instead of skimming.
* Understand how I think, not just what I built.
* Remember the experience after leaving.
* Feel confident that I can own products from discovery to launch.
* Want to start a conversation or schedule an interview.

---

# Design North Star

If a feature is fun but doesn't support the story, remove it.

If a feature showcases product thinking while making the experience more enjoyable, keep it.

The portfolio should balance creativity with clarity. Every interaction should have a purpose, and every animation should reinforce the story being told.
